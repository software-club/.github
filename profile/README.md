# Software Club 🚀

**Software Club** is an open community for students and aspiring software professionals who want more than just classroom theory.

We meet **weekly** to discuss the latest tech developments, sharpen real-world skills, and connect students with the people already doing the work in industry.

---

## 🎯 Our Mission

We exist to **bridge the gap between academic learning and industry experience**.

Universities teach the fundamentals — we teach you what happens on day one of the job. Our sessions are practical, conversational, and built around the problems engineers actually face.

---

## 🗓️ What We Do

### 💬 Weekly Tech Discussions
Stay current. Every week we cover recent developments in software engineering, tools, frameworks, and industry trends — no textbook required.

### 🛠️ Practical, Not Theoretical
We focus on how things work in practice. Expect live demos, real codebases, real workflows, and honest conversations about what it's actually like to work in tech.

### 🎤 Interview Prep
Whether you're aiming for an internship or your first full-time role, we run mock interviews, walk through common coding challenges, and share strategies that work.

### 🖥️ Presentation Prep
Learn how to communicate your ideas clearly and confidently. We help members prepare and deliver technical presentations — a skill that matters everywhere.

### 🎙️ Interviews with Real Software Engineers
We bring in working professionals — developers, engineers, tech leads — for candid interviews and Q&A sessions. Hear directly from people in the field about their journeys, day-to-day work, and advice for breaking in.

---

## 🤝 Who Is This For?

Software Club is for **students at any stage** — whether you've written your first line of code or you're about to graduate and step into the industry. If you want to learn, connect, and grow, you belong here.

---

## 📬 Get Involved

Keep an eye on our GitHub for resources, session notes, and community projects. Show up, ask questions, and start building real relationships with people in tech.

> *Talk less theory. Do more.*
